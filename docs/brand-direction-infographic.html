Surfr — the product story

A one-page summary of what the website is supposed to feel like, who the hero is, and how to tell when copy is on or off-brand.

The single narrative line

"Every session is worth measuring —
and worth sharing with the people who get it."

measuring
Technical excellence & accuracy
every
Inclusivity. First jump and 30m record both count.
people who get it
Harbor the community
The three pillars
01
Harbor the community
A place riders return to, not just an app they record with. The kite world checks in here.
Kills
Loneliness on the water. Sessions disappearing into a camera roll.
02
Inclusivity
First session or 30m session — same map. Everyone has a stat that matters to them.
Kills
The intimidation of pro-only highlight reels.
03
Technical excellence
Measured, not estimated. Numbers a rider would defend in court. Engineering as care.
Kills
Vague "fitness app" vibes. Untrustworthy data.
The pillars are in productive tension. Leaderboards risk excluding new riders. Pure inclusivity dilutes the technical claim. The story job is to hold all three at once — the way Nike held "elite performance" and "everyone is an athlete" simultaneously.
Who is the hero

The rider is the hero. The app is the tool.

Off-brand · app-as-hero
  • Surfr tracks every jump.
  • The app you need on the water.
  • Built for kitesurfers.
On-brand · rider-as-hero
  • Know it happened.
  • Find your tribe by the numbers.
  • Don't miss the day.
Slop swaps

Specifics defeat slop.

"Built by riders, for riders"
→
A founder photo + the year you started
"Don't take our word for it"
→
A real quote, named, with a real spot
"All-in-one app"
→
The one job this section actually does
"99% of active users"
→
One real customer or no testimonial at all
"Advanced algorithms"
→
The algorithm's name. ±height accuracy. 1Hz GPS.
"Seamlessly", "effortlessly", "powerful"
→
Delete. Use the noun. Trust the verb.
Page story jobs

Every page does one job.

Home
Frame the world. Promise the feeling."Surfr is where my session belongs."
Features
Prove technical excellence with stories, not specs."These people are obsessed with getting it right."
Spots
Make the map feel inhabited."Real riders have been here. I want to be next."
Leaderboard
Progression for everyone — not a pro highlights reel."There's a board where I count."
Roadmap
Trust through transparency."They ship in the open. They listen."
Team
The why behind the why."Built by people who actually ride."
Pro / Pricing
Value, not feature gates."Plus pays for itself in one good day."
Pre-publish test

Six questions before any copy ships.

1
Does it serve the single narrative line?
2
Does it pass the slop list?
3
Is the rider the hero, not the app?
4
Is there a specific, real, textured detail in here?
5
Would a first-time rider feel welcome reading this?
6
Could a competitor's brand team have written this exact sentence?
